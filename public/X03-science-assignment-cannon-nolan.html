Hailstones are formed when raindrops are carried by thunderstorm updrafts into extremely cold areas of the atmosphere which causes the drops to freeze. Hailstones grow when liquid drops collide with the frozen drops. When the liquid drops collide, they flash freeze onto the surface of the frozen drop. When the liquid drops flash freeze it also traps a little air causing cloudiness this is why hail is white instead of clear. once the hailstone becomes too heavy for the updraft to support it falls to the ground.

Hail is created in almost all severe thunderstorms but most of the time it melts before hitting the ground. however, in multi-cell thunderstorms many hailstones are produced but they are not very large because the mature stage the storm is short. In contrast supercell storms can produce big hail because they have stronger updrafts to lift the hail higher so they can accumulate more layers.

there are three basic ingredients needed to form hail: An adequate updraft, sufficient supercooled water, and a piece of ice or dust for it to grow on.

Air planes are not safe during hailstorms, one of the few big crashes happened“On April 4, 1977, a Southern Airways DC-9 crashed in New Hope, Georgia. Both engines of the plane ingested hail and lost thrust. The plane crashed onto the road and burst into flames.
